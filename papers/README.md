# Downloaded Papers

This directory contains the PDF corpus for the neural-symbolic learning resource phase. PDFs are stored directly in `papers/`; chunked 3-page reading copies and manifests are in `papers/pages/`.

## Core Method and Framework Papers

1. [Scallop: A Language for Neurosymbolic Programming](2304.04812_scallop_language_for_neurosymbolic_programming.pdf)
   - Authors: Ziyang Li, Jiani Huang, Mayur Naik
   - Year: 2023
   - Source: arXiv/PLDI, https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.04812
   - Why relevant: Datalog-based differentiable reasoning framework with Python/PyTorch integration and benchmark suite.

2. [DeepProbLog: Neural Probabilistic Logic Programming](1805.10872_deepproblog_neural_probabilistic_logic_programming.pdf)
   - Authors: Robin Manhaeve, Sebastijan Dumancic, Angelika Kimmig, Thomas Demeester, Luc De Raedt
   - Year: 2018
   - Source: arXiv/NeurIPS, https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.10872
   - Why relevant: Foundational neural predicate integration with probabilistic logic programs.

3. [Logic Tensor Networks](2012.13635_logic_tensor_networks.pdf)
   - Authors: Luciano Serafini, Artur d'Avila Garcez, Marco Gori, and collaborators
   - Year: 2020
   - Source: arXiv, https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.13635
   - Why relevant: Differentiable first-order fuzzy logic framework using Real Logic as a learning objective.

4. [LTNtorch: PyTorch implementation of Logic Tensor Networks](2409.16045_ltntorch_pytorch_logic_tensor_networks.pdf)
   - Authors: Tommaso Carraro and collaborators
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: arXiv, https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.16045
   - Why relevant: Practical PyTorch implementation of LTN, useful as a lightweight baseline.

5. [Neural Logic Machines](1904.11694_neural_logic_machines.pdf)
   - Authors: Honghua Dong, Jiayuan Mao, Tian Lin, Chong Wang, Lihong Li, Denny Zhou
   - Year: 2019
   - Source: arXiv/ICLR, https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.11694
   - Why relevant: Tensorized lifted-rule learning with strong systematic generalization experiments.

6. [The Neuro-Symbolic Concept Learner](1904.12584_neuro_symbolic_concept_learner.pdf)
   - Authors: Jiayuan Mao, Chuang Gan, Pushmeet Kohli, Joshua B. Tenenbaum, Jiajun Wu
   - Year: 2019
   - Source: arXiv/ICLR, https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.12584
   - Why relevant: Object-centric visual concept learning plus symbolic program execution on CLEVR.

7. [A-NeSI: A Scalable Approximate Method for Probabilistic Neurosymbolic Inference](2212.12393_a_nesi_approximate_neurosymbolic_inference.pdf)
   - Authors: Emile van Krieken, Thiviyan Thanapalasingam, Jakub M. Tomczak, Frank van Harmelen, Annette ten Teije
   - Year: 2022/2023
   - Source: arXiv/NeurIPS, https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.12393
   - Why relevant: Approximate inference for probabilistic neurosymbolic learning with polynomial-time scaling.

8. [DeepStochLog: Neural Stochastic Logic Programming](2106.12574_deepstochlog_neural_stochastic_logic_programming.pdf)
   - Authors: Robin Manhaeve, Giuseppe Marra, Angelika Kimmig, Luc De Raedt
   - Year: 2021
   - Source: arXiv, https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.12574
   - Why relevant: Stochastic logic programming alternative to possible-world inference in DeepProbLog-style systems.

9. [Logical Neural Networks](2006.13155_logical_neural_networks.pdf)
   - Authors: IBM Research team
   - Year: 2020
   - Source: arXiv, https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.13155
   - Why relevant: Interpretable neural units corresponding to formulas in weighted real-valued logic.

10. [Lobster: A GPU-Accelerated Framework for Neurosymbolic Programming](2503.21937_lobster_gpu_accelerated_neurosymbolic_programming.pdf)
    - Authors: Paul Biberstein, Ziyang Li, Joseph Devietti, Mayur Naik
    - Year: 2025
    - Source: arXiv, https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.21937
    - Why relevant: GPU acceleration for Scallop-style Datalog neurosymbolic programs.

11. [ANDRE: An Attention-based Neuro-symbolic Differentiable Rule Extractor](2605.04193_andre_differentiable_rule_extractor.pdf)
    - Authors: Iman Sharifi, Peng Wei, Saber Fallah
    - Year: 2026
    - Source: arXiv, https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04193
    - Why relevant: Recent differentiable ILP method with attention-based rule operators for noisy probabilistic data.

12. [Right for the Right Concept](stammer_2021_right_for_the_right_concept_nesy_xil.pdf)
    - Authors: Wolfgang Stammer, Patrick Schramowski, Kristian Kersting
    - Year: 2021
    - Source: CVPR, https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2021/html/Stammer_Right_for_the_Right_Concept_Revising_Neuro-Symbolic_Concepts_by_Interacting_CVPR_2021_paper.html
    - Why relevant: Concept-level explanatory interactive learning and CLEVR-Hans confounded visual reasoning benchmark.
